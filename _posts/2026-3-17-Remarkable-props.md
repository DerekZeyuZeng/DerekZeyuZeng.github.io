**March 17**. (Huang, Huh, Michałek, B. Wang, S. Wang; arXiv:2505.08881)

Theorem 1. For any $(p_{12}, p_{13}, p_{14}, p_{23}, p_{24}, p_{34}) \in \R^6_{\ge 0}$, the following propositions are equivalent:

1. Exists convex body $A\subseteq \R^4$, such that $p_{ij} = \mathrm{Area}\big(\pi_{ij}(A)\big)$, where $\pi_{ij}: \R^4 \to \R^2$ is the coordinate projection to $\mathrm{Span}\{e_i,e_j\}$ plane.

2. There exists a Euclidean triangle with side length $\sqrt{p_{12}p_{34}}$, $\sqrt{p_{13}p_{24}}$, and $\sqrt{p_{14}p_{23}}$, i.e., $(p_{ij}) \in \Delta(\mathbb{T_2})$

Furthermore, if we add the condition of a smooth convex body in 1, then the Euclidean triangle in 2 should be nondegenerate. Projectivily, we may use the Grassmannian $\mathrm{Gr}(2,4)(\mathbb T_2) \cong \mathbb P\big(\Delta(\mathbb T_2) \setminus \{0\}\big)$ instead of $\Delta(\mathbb T_2)$, which gives the projective class of $(p_{ij})$ up to positive rescaling.
